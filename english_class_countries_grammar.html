Vocabulary

Countries & Cities

We use city names to talk about where people are from, then say the country:

She's from Zurich. She's from Switzerland.
He's from Paris. He's from France.
I'm from Rome. I'm from Italy.
You're from Miami. You're from the United States.
She's from Moscow. She's from Russia.
He's from Istanbul. He's from Turkey.
I'm from Warsaw. I'm from Poland.
You're from Cairo. You're from Egypt.
She's from Berlin. She's from Germany.
Grammar

Verb "be" — he, she, it

Use He's / She's / It's for singular subjects:

She's
for women / girls
He's
for men / boys
It's
for things / places
She's from the United States. (a woman)
It's from China. (an object)
He's from Germany. (a man)
Grammar

Is / Isn't — questions & negatives

Is Paulo from Spain? — No, he isn't. He is from Brazil.
Where is Oaxaca? Is it in Mexico? — Yes, it is.
Is Robert Downey Jr from the USA? — Yes, he is. He's from New York.
Is Kobe in China? — No, it isn't. It's in Japan.
